# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Curious adults, students, educators, artists, and technologists encountering the experience on an iPad or large touchscreen. They want a more vivid way to think about artificial intelligence than a chat interface or product demonstration can offer.

## Product Purpose

Forms of Intelligence is a tactile visual essay. It uses touch, motion, voice, and illustration to make intelligence feel material: something that can gather, dissolve, grow, smell, swarm, and take new forms. Success means a reader leaves with a larger idea of intelligence and a stronger sense of agency about what we choose to build.

## Positioning

An essay about AI that behaves like its subject: alive under the hand, always reforming, and impossible to reduce to a chatbox.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary CTA: Begin touching the particle field.
- Secondary CTA: Listen to each movement as it appears.
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: What is the shape of intelligence?
- Belief ladder: intelligence has many natural forms; machine intelligence begins in human and physical matter; new senses can make machine intelligence genuinely alien; imagination and intent determine whether the result feels magical or disposable.
- Proof on hand: the experience is an exploratory prototype; it does not make a commercial or production claim.

## Brand Personality

Tactile, lucid, and uncanny. The voice combines scientific precision with the conviction of a manifesto. It should evoke wonder without mysticism, and urgency without fearmongering.

## Anti-references

Never resemble a chatbot, a SaaS landing page, neon-purple cyberpunk, a circuit-board brain, generic AI gradient art, a deck of explanatory cards, or low-effort generated “slop.” Avoid treating intelligence as a single ladder with humans at the top.

## Design Principles

1. Let the reader disturb the idea with their hands.
2. Show transformations instead of decorating explanations.
3. Give biological, animal, collective, and machine intelligence equal visual dignity.
4. Make each movement feel materially different while preserving one narrative rhythm.
5. End with human responsibility, not technological inevitability.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Maintain readable contrast and comfortable type sizes on tablet. Every touch interaction must also work with mouse and keyboard. Respect reduced-motion preferences, provide narration as an optional enhancement rather than a requirement, and keep the full essay legible without animation, audio, or color alone.
